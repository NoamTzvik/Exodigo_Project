Task: Field Team Lead
Research: The initial phase of the task requires thorough research on global
infrastructure. Focus on the following key areas:
• How underground infrastructure is organized and constructed worldwide.
• Current methodologies used for laying and maintaining underground
infrastructure.
• Key challenges faced in infrastructure projects globally, including regulatory,
technological, and environmental constraints.
Site Planning: You will lead a team on-site at "Pittsburgh" (40.244793, -77.171278) and
work closely with the mapping team in Israel. Your objective is to create a
comprehensive map of all infrastructure present at the Pittsburgh site.
1. Site Planning:
• The entire site must be scanned within one week.
• Each polygon in the scan must not exceed 35x40 meters and must
overlap with adjacent polygons by at least 2 meters.
• The site terrain is on an incline.
2. Scanning Methods:
• Each scan must utilize all three available sensor types and be conducted
in both horizontal and vertical orientations:
• Electro Magnetic: Requires transmitters placed 30-60 meters apart
from the polygon, with a 4-meter distance between each
transmitter.
• MD: The easiest sensor to scan with but the lowest priority.
• IDS: The slowest scanning method, utilizing a heavy system and
physically the most demanding.
3. Weather Conditions:
• Weather conditions are generally favorable, except for Wednesday
morning, when snow is expected, stopping later in the day.
4. Team Meetings:
• On Monday, all employees have weekly meetings at 9:00 AM.
• You have two morning meetings at 7:30 AM on Tuesday and Thursday.
5. Scanning Equipment:
• The system can operate only with 15 transmitters, you need to choose
how many transmitters you need for the project.
6. Travel Distance:
• The site is a 20-minute drive from your AB&B house.
7. Manpower:
• You need to decide how many field operators you need to finish the
project on time.
8. Road Closures:
• Consider road closures when planning the polygons.
9. Additional Tasks:
• During the scanning process, you will also need to mark key points in the
field for the mapping team. These points should be chosen based on their
importance in aiding accurate mapping and infrastructure analysis.
10. Post-Scan Planning:
• Ensure all necessary steps are taken to successfully complete the project
and finalize all required tasks before leaving the site.
